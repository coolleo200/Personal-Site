1. Cats have a smaller ecological footprint than dogs.

It's true, in 2009 "the new scientist" did a report on a new book by Robert and Brenda Vale titled "Time to Eat the Dog: The Real Guide to Sustainable Living". In the book the author estimated the carbon footprint of many household pets. the author argued that a medium sized dog had the same ecological foot print per a year as two standard sized "SUV's", only based on how much meat the dog eats per a year. The reason why dogs eating so much meat is a problem is because of the enormous amount of resources required to produce it. Cats, being smaller eat less meat therefore only making about the same ecological footprint as a small Volkswagen, the authors argued.- still quite a bit but, much better than your average dog.
